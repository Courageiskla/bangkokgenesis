What are Dermal Fillers?

Dermal fillers are gel-like substances, most commonly made of Hyaluronic Acid (HA), that are skillfully injected beneath the skin. HA is a naturally occurring substance in our body that keeps skin hydrated and volumized. As we age, our natural HA diminishes, leading to volume loss and wrinkles. Fillers work by replenishing this lost volume, providing immediate and visible results.

Preparation

  • ✓ Avoid blood-thinning medications and alcohol for several days prior.
  • ✓ Inform your doctor of any allergies or medical conditions.
  • ✓ It is best to schedule your appointment at least 2 weeks before any major event.

Aftercare

  • ✓ Avoid touching or pressing on the treated area for at least 6 hours.
  • ✓ Apply a cold compress to reduce minor swelling or bruising.
  • ✓ Avoid intense heat and strenuous exercise for 24-48 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most modern fillers contain a local anesthetic (lidocaine) to minimize discomfort. We can also apply a topical numbing cream before the procedure to ensure you are comfortable.

The longevity of fillers varies depending on the product used, the area treated, and your individual metabolism. Typically, results can last anywhere from 6 to 18 months.

Common side effects are temporary and mild, including redness, swelling, tenderness, and bruising at the injection site. These typically resolve within a few days. Serious complications are very rare when performed by an experienced medical injector.
